Welcome to the Bachelor Pool

Predict the order of finish of the before the season starts, using the information on the official ABC site.

As the season progresses, we calculate how closely your predictions compare to the actual order of finish.

Whoever’s rankings are closest to the actual order of finish wins the prize money and title belt.

To participate, fill out the  entry form  and submit $5 on Venmo before noon on the day of the season premier.

Entries will not be accepted if they (or the associated Venmo) arrive after noon on the day of the season premiere.

The entry form asks participants to rank all the contestants on the Bachelor/Bachelorette, from winner to first eliminated. Scores are calculated by assessing the correlation between every entry’s rankings and the actual order of finish. The closer the predicted rank of each contestant to the actual order of finish, the better one’s score.

Scores can range from -1.00 to +1.00; mathematically, +1.00 means that the entry got the order completely right, while -1.00 means that the entry got the order completely backwards (had the winner ranked last). There is one adjustment to the scores: a bonus of 0.10 is added to each entry that correctly ranked the winning contestant first. The highest possible score is +1.10.

In actuality, the order of finish on the show is never smooth – often, several contestants are eliminated at once. In such cases, simultaneously-eliminated contestants are assigned the average score for that group – for example, if two contestants are eliminated together in 23rd and 24th, they are both assigned an ‘actual’ rank of 23.5. Because of these simultaneous eliminations, it is not possible to predict the rank order exactly correctly (because your entry can’t account for these ties).

During the season, projected standings can be calculated each week using the order of roses as a stand-in for rankings for those who haven’t been eliminated (i.e. the contestant who receives the first rose is ranked first). As the weeks progress, the standings crystallize as more contestants are eliminated and locked into actual rankings. The final standings are calculated and published following the season’s finale. The individual(s) with the highest scores – including the bonus – receive prizes.

Disclaimer: The Bachelor/ette likes to throw viewers for a loop from time to time by deviating from the usual structure of the show. For example, new contestants may appear midseason and cannot be accounted for in the rankings. Other times, contestants will be eliminated, only to return later. For the purposes of this competition, every effort is made to adhere to the structured format, but the Bachelor Pool team has the final say in determining the order of elimination / rankings. No refunds, and decisions are final.
