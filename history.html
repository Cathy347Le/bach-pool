History

In 2011, a group of five friends in Washington DC began watching The Bachelor every Monday night. To make the viewing more interesting, they organized a simple fantasy draft: they would pause after the contestants were introduced during the first episode of each season, and draft a ‘team’ to follow throughout the season.

As members of this original group moved away and other friends were drawn in, the number of fantasy players expanded. Girlfriends got their boyfriends hooked on the show, housemates got their friends addicted, and soon the group was too large to hold a meaningful fantasy draft. The group adjusted the format of the predictions, but maintained the spirit of competition. Now spread across the country, from San Francisco to Chicago to Pennsylvania, the original crew has expanded the competition to include friends, relatives, coworkers, and anyone else who would like to join.

In 2019, the Bachelor Pool upgraded its website and data visualizations to a more professional level.
